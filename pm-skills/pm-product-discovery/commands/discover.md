# Discover

Run a complete product discovery workflow using Teresa Torres's Continuous Discovery framework.

## Step 1: Define the Desired Outcome

Ask the user: "What metric or behavior do you want to move this quarter? Be specific."

If they haven't provided one, prompt:
- What does success look like in 90 days?
- Which business metric matters most right now?
- What user behavior change would make the biggest difference?

Frame the outcome as: "Increase/decrease [metric] by [X%] by [date]"

## Step 2: Build the Opportunity Space

Activate the **opportunity-solution-tree** skill.

Map 5–8 customer opportunities (needs, pain points, desires) that could move the outcome.
Use Jobs-to-be-Done format: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."

Cluster and prioritize opportunities using Reach × Impact × Confidence.

## Step 3: Generate Solutions

For the top 2 opportunity clusters, generate 3–5 solutions each.
Range from small quick-win experiments to larger strategic bets.

## Step 4: Map Assumptions

Activate the **identify-assumptions** skill.

For each solution, surface the top 3 risky assumptions across the five categories (Value, Usability, Feasibility, Viability, GTM).

Prioritize assumptions using the Risk × Knowledge matrix.

## Step 5: Design Experiments

For the top 5 assumptions, design lightweight experiments:

| Assumption | Experiment Type | What We'll Build | Success Metric | Time |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|------|
| ...        | Interview / Prototype / Smoke test / A/B | ... | ... | [days] |

## Step 6: Deliver the Discovery Brief

Produce a one-page summary:
- **Desired Outcome**: [Statement]
- **Top Opportunity**: [Cluster name + why it's highest priority]
- **Recommended Solutions**: [Top 3]
- **Riskiest Assumptions**: [Top 5, ranked]
- **First Experiment**: [What to run this sprint]
- **Success Criteria**: [How we know the experiment worked]
