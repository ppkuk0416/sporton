# Interview Script

Use when: the user wants to conduct user interviews, customer discovery sessions, or jobs-to-be-done research. Generates a ready-to-use interview guide.

Trigger: "interview script", "user interview", "customer research", "discovery interview", "talk to users"

## Framework (Teresa Torres — Continuous Discovery)

Effective discovery interviews focus on past behavior, not hypothetical opinions.

### Core Principles
1. **Ask about the past** — "Tell me about the last time you..." not "Would you..."
2. **Follow the story** — Probe for specifics: "And then what happened?"
3. **No leading questions** — Never hint at your solution
4. **Silence is data** — Let pauses breathe
5. **Ask "why" three times** — Surface underlying motivations

### Interview Structure (45–60 min)

#### Opening (5 min)
- Introduce yourself and purpose (never mention the product you're building)
- "We're trying to understand how people [domain], not to sell anything"
- Ask for permission to record/take notes

**Warm-up questions:**
- "Tell me a bit about your role and what you work on day-to-day."
- "How long have you been doing [relevant activity]?"

#### Context Setting (10 min)
Surface their world before diving into the problem space.
- "Walk me through your typical [day/week] when it comes to [domain]."
- "What tools or methods do you currently use for [activity]?"
- "What does success look like for you in [domain]?"

#### Story Mining (20–25 min)
The most important section. Get them to tell specific stories.
- "Tell me about the last time you had to [core job]."
- "Take me back to that moment. What were you doing right before?"
- "What did you actually do? Walk me through step by step."
- "What was the hardest part of that?"
- "What happened after that?"
- "How did you feel about that outcome?"

Probe for:
- Workarounds ("What did you do when X didn't work?")
- Frequency ("How often does this come up?")
- Stakes ("What happens if you get this wrong?")
- Decision triggers ("What made you decide to do it that way?")

#### Closing (5–10 min)
- "Is there anything about [domain] that I haven't asked about that you think is important?"
- "Who else should I talk to about this topic?"
- "Is there anything you wish existed that would make your life easier here?"

#### Debrief (5 min — for yourself, not the interviewee)
Immediately after, note:
- Biggest surprise
- Strongest pain point mentioned
- Any phrase they used that was memorable
- Opportunity hypothesis formed

## Output Format

| Question | Purpose / What to Listen For |

Include opening, context, story mining, and closing sections. Add facilitator notes for each section. Mark required questions with ★ and optional probes with ○.
