# Brainstorm Ideas for New Products

Use when: the user wants to ideate on a new product, explore untapped market opportunities, or generate a diverse set of ideas for a product that doesn't yet exist.

Trigger: "brainstorm new product", "ideate from scratch", "what could we build", "new product ideas"

## Framework

Apply four lenses to generate a broad, diverse idea space:

### Lens 1: Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)
Identify the functional, emotional, and social jobs customers want to accomplish.
- What are they trying to get done?
- What do they hire current solutions for?
- What workarounds are they using?

For each job, ask: "What product would make this job 10x easier?"

### Lens 2: Pain-First Ideation
List the top 5 frustrations in the target domain. For each frustration:
- Severity: 1–10 (how painful is this?)
- Frequency: daily / weekly / monthly
- Current fix: What do people do today?
- Idea: A product that eliminates this pain entirely

### Lens 3: Technology Enablement
Identify 3–5 technology shifts (AI, mobile, APIs, regulation changes) that open new product possibilities. For each shift:
- What used to be impossible that's now easy?
- Who benefits most?
- What product becomes obvious?

### Lens 4: Analogy Transfer
Pick 3 successful products from adjacent industries. For each:
- What's the core mechanism that makes it work?
- How would that mechanism apply to your target domain?
- What product would result?

## Idea Scoring

For each idea generated, rate on three dimensions (1–5):
- **Desirability**: Do people want this?
- **Feasibility**: Can we build it?
- **Viability**: Can we monetize it?

Top ideas = highest combined score with no dimension below 3.

## Output Format

Produce at minimum 10 ideas organized by lens. For each idea:
- **Idea name**: [2–4 words]
- **One-liner**: [What it does in one sentence]
- **Target customer**: [Who benefits most]
- **Key insight**: [The aha moment — why this works]
- **Score**: D[x] F[x] V[x]

End with a ranked shortlist of the top 3 ideas with a brief rationale.
