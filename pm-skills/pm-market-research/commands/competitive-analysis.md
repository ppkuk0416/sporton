# Competitive Analysis

Build a complete competitive analysis for your product.

## Step 1: Define the Competitive Set

If not provided, ask:
- What is your product and target customer?
- Who are the top 3–5 direct competitors?
- Any indirect competitors or substitutes to include?
- What decisions will this analysis inform? (pricing / positioning / roadmap / investor pitch)

## Step 2: Run the Analysis

Activate the **competitor-analysis** skill.

For each competitor, complete the full profile covering: overview, positioning, product, pricing, distribution, customer sentiment, and strategic vulnerabilities.

## Step 3: Build the Feature Matrix

Create a side-by-side feature comparison for the top 10–15 capabilities that matter most to the target customer.

Highlight where you have a clear advantage (✅) and where you have a gap (❌).

## Step 4: Identify White Space

From the positioning map, identify:
- Underserved positions in the market
- Feature gaps no competitor has filled
- Customer segments underserved by existing solutions

## Step 5: Strategic Recommendations

Based on the full analysis, deliver 5 recommendations:
1. Where to compete head-on (your strength vs. their weakness)
2. Where to avoid competing (their strength vs. your weakness)
3. White space opportunity to move into
4. Features to build to close the most critical gaps
5. Messaging to adopt based on competitive positioning

## Step 6: Deliver Competitive Brief

One-page competitive summary:
- Landscape overview (3 sentences)
- Our strongest differentiators vs. each competitor
- Our biggest vulnerability
- Recommended strategic moves
- Competitive battlecard summary (for sales team)
