# User Personas

Use when: the user wants to define target customer segments, create personas for design and development alignment, or communicate customer understanding to stakeholders.

Trigger: "user persona", "customer persona", "who is our user", "target customer profile", "buyer persona"

## Framework

### Persona Principles (Alan Cooper / Jobs-to-be-Done)

Good personas are based on:
- **Behavioral data**: What users actually do, not what they say they do
- **Goals and motivations**: Why they do it
- **Pain points**: What frustrates them in reaching their goals
- **Context**: When, where, and how they use the product

Avoid:
- Demographic-only personas ("35-year-old female marketing manager") — too vague
- Made-up personas without research — misleading and dangerous
- Too many personas — focus on the 1–2 primary ones that drive 80% of decisions

### Persona Validation

For each persona, rate data confidence:
- 🟢 High confidence: Based on 10+ interviews or strong analytics data
- 🟡 Medium confidence: Based on 3–9 interviews or partial data
- 🔴 Low confidence: Hypothetical — needs validation

## Output Format

Generate 2–3 primary personas using the template above. For each:
1. Full persona card (all fields populated)
2. Confidence rating and data sources
3. "A day in the life" narrative (2–3 paragraphs)
4. Key product implications: What this persona requires, and what would break for them

End with a persona priority matrix: which persona to design for first and why.
