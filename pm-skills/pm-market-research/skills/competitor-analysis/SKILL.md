# Competitor Analysis

Use when: the user wants to understand the competitive landscape, identify differentiation opportunities, prepare a competitive battlecard, or inform a strategy or pricing decision.

Trigger: "competitor analysis", "competitive landscape", "compare competitors", "who are our competitors", "competitive research"

## Framework

### Step 1: Competitor Taxonomy

Classify competitors into three categories:
- **Direct**: Same target customer, same solution type
- **Indirect**: Same target customer, different solution type
- **Substitutes**: Different approach but competes for the same budget/attention

Analyze direct competitors deeply (3–5); surface-level for indirect and substitutes (1–2 each).

### Step 2: Competitive Profile (Per Competitor)

```
COMPETITOR: [Name]
Type: Direct / Indirect / Substitute
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Overview:
• Founded / HQ / Funding / Team size (if known)
• Business model: [How they make money]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### Step 3: Feature Comparison Matrix

Build a matrix of the top 10–15 features across all competitors:

| Feature | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|---------|----|----|----|----|
| [Feature] | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ | 🔶 (partial) |

Legend: ✅ Full support | 🔶 Partial | ❌ Not available

### Step 4: Positioning Map

Plot competitors on a 2×2 matrix using the two most strategically relevant dimensions for your market:
- Examples: Ease of use vs. Power / Price vs. Feature depth / Enterprise vs. SMB focus

Identify white space: areas of the map with no strong competitor.

### Step 5: Strategic Implications

From the analysis, answer:
1. Where are we strongest vs. competitors?
2. Where are we most vulnerable?
3. What's the clearest white space opportunity?
4. Which competitor poses the highest threat in the next 12 months?
5. What should we do differently based on this analysis?

## Output Format

1. Competitor taxonomy (table)
2. Competitor profiles (full template per competitor)
3. Feature comparison matrix
4. Positioning map (described or ASCII-rendered)
5. Strategic implications (5 bullet points)
6. Win/loss patterns: Why we win vs. each competitor, why we lose
