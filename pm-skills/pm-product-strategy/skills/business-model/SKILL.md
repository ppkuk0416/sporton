# Business Model

Use when: the user wants to map how their product creates, delivers, and captures value; explore different monetization approaches; or stress-test their current business model.

Trigger: "business model", "business model canvas", "lean canvas", "how do we make money", "monetization model", "revenue model"

## Framework

### Part 1: Business Model Canvas (9 Blocks)

**Customer Segments** — Who are your most important customers?
- Mass market, niche market, segmented, diversified, multi-sided platform?
- List 2–3 primary segments with defining characteristics

**Value Propositions** — What value do you deliver to each segment?
- What problem are you solving? What need are you satisfying?
- Why do customers choose you over alternatives?

**Channels** — How do you reach customers?
- Awareness → Evaluation → Purchase → Delivery → After-sales
- Own channels vs. partner channels

**Customer Relationships** — What relationship does each segment expect?
- Self-service / assisted / automated / community / co-creation

**Revenue Streams** — How does the product generate revenue?
- One-time payment / subscription / usage-based / freemium / licensing / referral fee
- For each stream: volume, margin, and strategic importance

**Key Resources** — What do you need to make the model work?
- Physical / intellectual / human / financial

**Key Activities** — What do you do most importantly?
- Production / problem-solving / platform management

**Key Partners** — Who do you need to work with?
- Strategic alliances, suppliers, JV partners, coopetition

**Cost Structure** — What are the most important costs?
- Cost-driven vs. value-driven
- Fixed costs, variable costs, economies of scale/scope

### Part 2: Lean Canvas (Problem-Focused)

For early-stage products, complete the Lean Canvas instead:
1. Problem (top 3 customer problems)
2. Solution (top 3 features)
3. Unique Value Proposition (single, clear, compelling message)
4. Unfair Advantage (can't be easily copied)
5. Customer Segments + Early Adopters
6. Key Metrics (what you measure)
7. Channels
8. Revenue Streams
9. Cost Structure

### Part 3: Business Model Stress Test

Ask these questions of the model:
- What happens if the main revenue stream drops 30%?
- Which key partner, if lost, would break the model?
- What's the CAC:LTV ratio, and is it sustainable?
- Where is the model most vulnerable to competition?
- What would a scaled version of this look like?

## Output Format

1. **Business Model Canvas** — 9-block summary table
2. **Revenue Model Deep-dive** — For each revenue stream: mechanism, pricing rationale, target margin
3. **Stress Test Results** — Top 3 vulnerabilities with mitigation options
4. **Business Model Score** (1–10 on: differentiation, scalability, defensibility, unit economics)
5. **Recommended experiments** — What to validate before scaling
