# Value Proposition

Use when: the user needs to articulate why their product exists, what problem it solves, and why customers should choose it over alternatives.

Trigger: "value proposition", "why us", "product positioning", "what value do we deliver", "differentiation"

## Framework

### Part 1: Customer Profile (JTBD)

Map the three layers of customer needs:

**Jobs** — What are they trying to accomplish?
- Functional jobs: practical tasks ("manage my team's sprints")
- Emotional jobs: how they want to feel ("feel in control of my product")
- Social jobs: how they want to be perceived ("look data-driven to stakeholders")

**Pains** — What frustrates them today?
- Undesired outcomes: what goes wrong
- Obstacles: what stops them from getting the job done
- Risks: what they fear losing

**Gains** — What would delight them?
- Required gains: minimum expectations
- Expected gains: what they assume a solution has
- Desired gains: nice-to-haves they'd love
- Unexpected gains: surprises that create loyalty

### Part 2: Value Map

Map your product's response to each customer layer:

**Products & Services** — What you offer (not features — outcomes)

**Pain Relievers** — How you eliminate, reduce, or minimize specific pains
- For each pain: "Our product relieves this by..."

**Gain Creators** — How you create specific gains for customers
- For each gain: "Our product creates this by..."

### Part 3: Fit Assessment

**Problem-Solution Fit**: Do our pain relievers address real pains? (not assumed ones)
**Product-Market Fit**: Do our gain creators match what customers actually value?

Rate each pain reliever and gain creator: 🔴 No fit / 🟡 Partial fit / 🟢 Strong fit

### Part 4: Value Proposition Statement

Use this template:
```
We help [customer segment]
who want to [job to be done]
by [key differentiating action]
unlike [alternative/competitor]
which means [outcome for customer]
```

## Output Format

1. **Customer Profile** — Jobs, Pains, Gains (bullet list per category)
2. **Value Map** — Pain relievers + gain creators mapped to each
3. **Fit Assessment** — Traffic light rating per element
4. **Value Proposition Statement** — 2–3 sentence version + one-liner
5. **Weak spots** — Where fit is 🔴 or 🟡, with recommendations
