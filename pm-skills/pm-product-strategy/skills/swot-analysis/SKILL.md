# SWOT Analysis

Use when: the user wants to assess their product or company's strategic position, prepare for planning cycles, or identify where to compete and where to avoid.

Trigger: "SWOT analysis", "strategic assessment", "strengths and weaknesses", "TOWS matrix", "competitive position"

## Framework

### Part 1: SWOT Quadrants

**Strengths** (Internal, positive)
- Unique capabilities or assets competitors lack
- Strong customer relationships or retention metrics
- Technical moats or proprietary data
- Brand, team, or distribution advantages

**Weaknesses** (Internal, negative)
- Resource gaps (people, capital, time)
- Product gaps or technical debt
- Weak areas relative to competitors
- Organizational constraints

**Opportunities** (External, positive)
- Market trends creating new demand
- Competitor vulnerabilities or exits
- Technology shifts enabling new approaches
- Regulatory changes opening markets
- Underserved customer segments

**Threats** (External, negative)
- New entrants or competitive moves
- Technology disruption to your model
- Customer behavior shifts
- Regulatory or macroeconomic risks
- Platform dependency risks

### Part 2: TOWS Matrix (Strategic Options)

Cross the SWOT quadrants to derive four strategic directions:

| | **Opportunities** | **Threats** |
|---|---|---|
| **Strengths** | SO: Use strengths to capture opportunities | ST: Use strengths to mitigate threats |
| **Weaknesses** | WO: Overcome weaknesses to access opportunities | WT: Minimize weaknesses AND threats |

For each cell, generate 2–3 concrete strategic options.

### Part 3: Prioritization

Rate each strategic option:
- **Urgency**: How time-sensitive is acting on this? (High / Medium / Low)
- **Impact**: What's the potential upside or risk reduction? (High / Medium / Low)
- **Feasibility**: Can we realistically execute this? (High / Medium / Low)

Top priorities = High urgency + High impact + High feasibility.

## Output Format

1. **SWOT Matrix** — 4-quadrant table with 5–8 items per quadrant
2. **TOWS Matrix** — 4-cell table with strategic options per combination
3. **Top 5 Strategic Priorities** — Ranked by urgency + impact + feasibility
4. **Key Insight** — The single most important strategic implication
5. **Recommended next review** — When to revisit this analysis
