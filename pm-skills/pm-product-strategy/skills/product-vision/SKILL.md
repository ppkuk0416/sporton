# Product Vision

Use when: the user wants to create or refine a product vision, align a team around a long-term direction, or communicate strategy to stakeholders.

Trigger: "product vision", "vision statement", "where are we going", "long-term product direction", "north star for the product"

## Framework

A great product vision is:
- **Inspiring** — Motivates the team even on hard days
- **Durable** — Relevant for 3–5 years, not next quarter
- **Customer-centric** — Describes the world you're creating for users
- **Specific enough** — Can be used to make strategic decisions

### Framework 1: Geoffrey Moore's Vision Template

```
For [target customer]
Who [has this need or opportunity]
The [product name] is a [product category]
That [key benefit / compelling reason to buy]
Unlike [primary competitive alternative]
Our product [primary differentiation statement]
```

### Framework 2: Marty Cagan — Inspiring Vision Components

A strong product vision answers:
1. **Who are we creating value for?** (Primary customer)
2. **What problem are we solving?** (Pain or aspiration, not feature)
3. **What does success look like for the customer?** (Desired outcome)
4. **What kind of company are we becoming?** (Strategic positioning)
5. **Why does this matter?** (The "why" — emotional hook)

### Framework 3: Amazon PR/FAQ (Working Backwards)

Write a fake press release as if the product has already succeeded:
- **Headline**: [Product name] [achieves what outcome] for [customers]
- **Subheading**: [One sentence elaboration]
- **Problem statement**: "Until now, [customer type] struggled with [problem]..."
- **Solution**: "[Product] solves this by..."
- **Customer quote**: Fictional quote from a delighted customer
- **Call to action**: What users do next

## Vision Assessment Criteria

Score your vision on:
- Clarity (1–5): Can anyone understand it in 30 seconds?
- Inspiration (1–5): Does it energize people?
- Durability (1–5): Will it still be relevant in 5 years?
- Decision utility (1–5): Does it help you say no to things?
- Customer centricity (1–5): Is the customer the hero?

Target: 20+ total. Below 15 = needs revision.

## Output Format

Present:
1. **Vision Statement** (one sentence, customer-centric)
2. **Vision Narrative** (3–5 sentences, Geoffrey Moore or PR/FAQ format)
3. **Strategic Implications** (3 things this vision says "no" to)
4. **Vision Score** (against five criteria)
5. **Suggested refinements** (if score < 20)
