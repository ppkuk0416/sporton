# Review Resume

Use when: the user wants their PM resume reviewed, improved, or optimized for job applications, ATS systems, or specific job descriptions.

Trigger: "review resume", "resume feedback", "improve my resume", "CV review", "PM resume", "resume critique"

## Framework

### 10 Best Practices for PM Resumes

#### 1. XYZ+S Formula (Google's recommended format)
Every bullet should follow: "Accomplished [X] by doing [Y], which resulted in [Z], as shown by [S (signal/proof)]."

Bad: "Led product development of mobile app"
Good: "Shipped new onboarding flow (Y) for mobile app that increased D7 retention by 18% (Z), contributing $2M ARR (S)"

#### 2. Quantify Every Impact
Rule: If it's not quantified, it doesn't count.
- Revenue impact: $X generated, $X saved
- User metrics: X% improvement, X users affected
- Time: Delivered X weeks ahead of schedule
- Scale: Grew from X to Y users

#### 3. Lead with Outcomes, Not Activities
Remove: "Responsible for," "Helped with," "Assisted in"
Replace with: Active verbs that show ownership (Led, Shipped, Grew, Reduced, Launched, Increased)

#### 4. ATS Keyword Optimization
Match keywords from job descriptions:
- Discovery: user research, customer interviews, opportunity mapping
- Strategy: roadmap, prioritization, OKRs, north star metric
- Execution: PRD, sprint planning, agile, scrum, stakeholder management
- Analytics: A/B testing, cohort analysis, data-driven, SQL, metrics

#### 5. Show PM Breadth: Discovery → Strategy → Execution → Launch
Ideal resume shows experience across the full PM lifecycle, not just execution.

#### 6. STAR Structure for Major Accomplishments
Situation → Task → Action → Result
Keep this to 2–3 lines per role, not just in cover letters.

#### 7. Social Proof and Signals
Include: promotions, awards, launches (with press coverage), team size managed, executive sponsors.

#### 8. PM-Specific Skills Section
Explicitly list: tools (JIRA, Figma, Amplitude, SQL, Notion), frameworks (JTBD, OKRs, OST), methodologies (agile, continuous discovery).

#### 9. Tailoring for the Target Role
Generic resumes fail. For each application:
- Match the exact language of the JD
- Lead with the experience most relevant to that role
- Use their company-specific metrics or categories when possible

#### 10. PM Resume Red Flags to Fix
- Engineering-heavy language with no user outcomes
- Descriptions of features, not impact
- No quantification
- Buzzword soup ("cross-functional," "strategic," "innovative") without specifics
- Activity lists ("attended," "participated") instead of ownership statements

## Output Format

For each resume section:
1. **Current score** (1–5): How well does it meet the 10 best practices?
2. **Issues found**: Specific problems with each bullet or section
3. **Rewritten version**: Improved bullets using the XYZ+S formula
4. **Missing elements**: What should be added

End with:
- Overall resume score (10 criteria × 5 = 50 max)
- Top 3 priorities to fix immediately
- ATS keyword gaps (if job description provided)
