# Battlecard

Create a complete competitive battlecard for sales and CS teams.

## Step 1: Gather Competitive Intelligence

If not provided, ask:
- Which competitor is this battlecard for?
- What do we know about their strengths?
- Where do we lose to them most often?
- Do we have any customer win/loss stories vs. this competitor?
- Any specific objections sales hears from customers comparing us?

## Step 2: Build the Battlecard

Activate the **competitive-battlecard** skill.

Generate the full battlecard using the template: one-line summary, their pitch, why we win, objection handling, trap-setting questions, where we lose, and proof points.

## Step 3: Sharpen the Objection Handling

For each major objection:
- Acknowledge what's true about the competitor claim
- Redirect to our strength without being defensive
- Add a concrete proof point (customer story, metric, feature comparison)

Test each response: Does it sound like something a confident salesperson would say? Not defensive, not dismissive.

## Step 4: Add Training Scenarios

Include 2–3 role-play scenarios:
- "The customer is leaning toward [Competitor] because of pricing — what do you say?"
- "The customer says [Competitor] has been around 10 years and we're a startup — how do you respond?"
- "The champion loves us but their boss prefers [Competitor] — what's your play?"

## Step 5: Format for Distribution

Produce the battlecard in a format ready for:
- Notion / Confluence page
- Printed one-pager
- Sales onboarding deck slide

Add footer: "Last updated: [date] | Owner: [PM or PMM name] | Feedback: [Slack channel]"
