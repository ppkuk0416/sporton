# Competitive Battlecard

Use when: the user wants to help their sales team win against a specific competitor, prepare for competitive deals, or train customer-facing teams on differentiation.

Trigger: "battlecard", "competitive battlecard", "win against competitor", "sales competitive", "how do we beat", "competitor objections"

## Framework

### Battlecard Structure (One per Competitor)

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
BATTLECARD: [Our Product] vs [Competitor]
Target audience: Sales, CS, SE teams
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ONE-LINE SUMMARY
"[Competitor] is [characterization]. We're better for [ICP] because [core differentiator]."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
THEIR PITCH (Know Your Enemy)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Their value proposition: [In their own words]
Their key strengths:
• [Strength 1]
• [Strength 2]
Their primary target customer: [Who they win with most]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
WHY WE WIN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Our strongest differentiators vs. them:
• [Differentiator 1]: [Evidence / proof point]
• [Differentiator 2]: [Evidence / proof point]
• [Differentiator 3]: [Evidence / proof point]

The customer profile where we consistently win:
[Description of ideal deal characteristics where we beat them]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
OBJECTION HANDLING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

"[Competitor] has been around longer and is more established."
→ [Response that acknowledges + redirects]

"[Competitor] is cheaper."
→ [Response focused on value, not price defense]

"[Competitor] has feature X that you don't."
→ [Response: acknowledge gap, explain your different approach or roadmap]

"We already use [Competitor] and switching is hard."
→ [Response: migration support, ROI of switching, switching cost minimizer]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TRAP-SETTING QUESTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Questions to ask that favor us:
1. "How important is [our strength] to your team?"
2. "How do you currently handle [their weakness]?"
3. "What happens when [scenario that breaks their product]?"

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
WHERE WE LOSE (Be Honest)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
We lose when:
• [Scenario 1 — e.g., customer needs X capability we don't have]
• [Scenario 2 — e.g., heavily budget-constrained deal]

When to walk away:
• [Red flag 1: Deal is not worth pursuing]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PROOF POINTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Customer switched from [Competitor] to us:
• [Company name or anonymized] — [Why they switched, outcome achieved]

Review quotes vs. [Competitor]:
• "[Verbatim or paraphrased positive review quote]"
```

## Output Format

Generate one complete battlecard per competitor using the template above. Each battlecard should be:
- Printable on one page (or one Notion/Confluence card)
- Written in the voice of a sales trainer (direct, confident, honest)
- Updated quarterly (note last-updated date)
- Linked to evidence sources (G2 reviews, win/loss data, customer interviews)
