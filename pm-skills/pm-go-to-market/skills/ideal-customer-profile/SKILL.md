# Ideal Customer Profile

Use when: the user wants to define their ICP, improve lead qualification, prioritize which customer segments to target, or align sales and marketing on who to focus on.

Trigger: "ideal customer profile", "ICP", "who is our ideal customer", "target customer", "lead qualification", "customer segmentation"

## Framework

### ICP vs. Persona

An **ICP** defines the ideal company or account (B2B) or customer type (B2C) — not the individual.
A **Persona** defines the individual within that company.

An ICP answers: "What kind of company or customer do we win with, expand with, and retain?"

### Part 1: ICP Dimensions (B2B)

#### Firmographics (Who they are)
- Industry / vertical: [Specific, not "tech" — be precise]
- Company size: [Employees or ARR range]
- Geography: [Target regions or countries]
- Business model: [B2B SaaS / B2C / Marketplace / etc.]
- Stage: [Startup / growth / enterprise]
- Funding status: [Bootstrapped / VC-backed / public]

#### Technographics (What they use)
- Current tools in the relevant space: [e.g., "Uses Salesforce, not HubSpot"]
- Technical sophistication: [No-code / developer-led / fully technical]
- Integration dependencies: [Must connect to X]

#### Behavioral Signals (How they buy and use)
- Growth rate: [Fast-growing vs stable]
- Hiring signals: [Hiring for roles that suggest need]
- Engagement: [Active on relevant forums, events]
- Buying trigger: [Event that causes them to seek a solution]

#### Psychographic / Cultural Fit
- Decision-making style: [Committee / champion-led / top-down]
- Attitude toward innovation: [Early adopter vs. wait-and-see]
- Success definition: [How they measure the value they get from tools]

### Part 2: ICP Scoring Model

Build a lead scoring model based on ICP fit:

| Dimension | Weight | Score (1–5) | Weighted Score |
|-----------|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Industry match | 25% | [1–5] | ... |
| Company size | 20% | [1–5] | ... |
| Technographic match | 20% | [1–5] | ... |
| Buying trigger present | 20% | [1–5] | ... |
| Budget signals | 15% | [1–5] | ... |
| **Total** | 100% | | [weighted avg] |

- Score > 4.0: Priority A — focus all energy here
- Score 3.0–4.0: Priority B — worth pursuing with standard process
- Score < 3.0: Disqualify or deprioritize

### Part 3: Negative ICP (Disqualifiers)

Define who is NOT a good fit:
- [Characteristic that consistently leads to churn or low expansion]
- [Size or segment where we can't deliver ROI]
- [Industry with regulatory or technical incompatibility]

### Part 4: ICP Validation

Analyze your top 20% of customers (by revenue, NPS, retention):
- What do they have in common?
- What did they look like before they bought?
- What trigger drove them to find us?

This reveals your actual ICP vs. your assumed one.

## Output Format

1. **ICP definition**: All four dimensions populated
2. **ICP scoring model**: Weighted table with scoring guidance
3. **Negative ICP**: Disqualifier list
4. **ICP one-liner**: "Our ideal customer is [X company type] that [key characteristic] and is experiencing [buying trigger]."
5. **ICP validation checklist**: What data to analyze to confirm this ICP with evidence
6. **Beachhead segment**: The single most attractive subset of the ICP to dominate first
