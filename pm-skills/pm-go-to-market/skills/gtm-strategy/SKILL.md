# GTM Strategy

Use when: the user is preparing a product launch, entering a new market, or needs to define how to reach and convert target customers.

Trigger: "go-to-market strategy", "GTM plan", "launch strategy", "how do we reach customers", "market entry", "product launch plan"

## Framework

### Part 1: Target Segment Definition

Before going to market, define the beachhead:
- **ICP** (Ideal Customer Profile): The single best-fit customer type
- **Beachhead segment**: The narrowest segment to dominate first before expanding
- **Early adopters**: The subset of ICP most likely to buy early (tech-forward, high pain, low switching cost)

Define the ICP along these dimensions:
- Company type: [Industry / size / stage / geography]
- Economic buyer: [Title / authority / budget ownership]
- End user: [Who actually uses the product day-to-day]
- Key trigger: [What event causes them to seek a solution now?]
- Disqualifiers: [Who is NOT a good fit and why]

### Part 2: GTM Motion Selection

Choose the primary GTM motion:

| Motion | Best For | Key Metric | Examples |
|--------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Inbound** | Educational content, SEO, thought leadership | MQLs, organic traffic | HubSpot, Drift |
| **Outbound** | Clear ICP, direct sales, enterprise | Meetings booked, pipeline | Salesforce, Outreach |
| **Product-Led** | Viral, self-serve, bottom-up | PQLs, activation rate | Slack, Figma, Notion |
| **Community** | Developer tools, creator economy | Community members, NPS | Twilio, GitHub |
| **Partnerships** | Distribution via existing platforms | Partner-sourced pipeline | Shopify apps, Zapier |
| **Paid** | Proven ICP, measurable CAC | ROAS, CAC payback | Most consumer apps |
| **Account-Based** | Enterprise, small total addressable market | ABM account penetration | Demandbase, 6sense |

Most companies use a primary motion + 1–2 secondary motions.

### Part 3: Positioning and Messaging

For the launch:
- **Category**: What category do you create or compete in?
- **Core message**: One sentence that captures the value for the ICP
- **Key proof point**: One compelling number or customer story
- **Differentiator**: Why you, not a competitor?

### Part 4: Channel Plan

For each acquisition channel:
```
Channel: [Name]
Motion type: [Inbound / Outbound / PLG / Paid / etc.]
Target audience: [Segment reached via this channel]
Estimated CAC: $[X]
Estimated volume: [X leads/month]
Time to results: [Weeks to first results]
Owner: [Who runs this channel]
```

### Part 5: Launch Sequencing

Phase 1 — Controlled beta (Weeks 1–4):
- Target: 20–50 friendly customers
- Goal: Validate product-market fit signals
- Success metric: [X% would be disappointed if product disappeared]

Phase 2 — Limited launch (Months 2–3):
- Target: 100–500 customers via primary channel
- Goal: Prove repeatable acquisition
- Success metric: CAC payback < [X months]

Phase 3 — Full launch (Month 4+):
- Expand channels, invest in what's working
- Goal: Grow to [revenue/users] milestone

## Output Format

1. **ICP + beachhead segment definition**
2. **Primary GTM motion recommendation** with rationale
3. **Channel plan** (table with CAC, volume, owner)
4. **Core positioning statement** for the launch
5. **Launch phasing plan** (Phase 1 → 2 → 3 with milestones)
6. **GTM risks** (top 3 things that could derail the launch)
