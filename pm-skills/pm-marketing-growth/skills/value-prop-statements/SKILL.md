# Value Prop Statements

Use when: the user needs to write value proposition copy for a specific context — landing page, sales deck, email campaign, onboarding flow, or investor pitch.

Trigger: "value prop statement", "marketing copy", "landing page copy", "headline", "tagline", "pitch", "messaging"

## Framework

A value proposition statement translates positioning into context-specific language. The same underlying value is expressed differently depending on audience and context.

### Context-Specific Templates

#### 1. Landing Page Headline
Audience: Curious visitors who don't know you yet.
Goal: Capture attention in 5 seconds and make them scroll.

Format: [Outcome] for [who] [in some differentiating way]

Examples:
- "Ship faster, break less" (Vercel)
- "The all-in-one workspace for your notes, tasks, wikis, and databases" (Notion)
- "The B2B payment solution that reduces invoice payment time by 30%" (fictional)

Tips:
- Lead with the outcome, not the product
- Be specific — quantify where you can
- Avoid jargon — if you need to explain it, it's not a headline

#### 2. Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)
Audience: Someone who just asked "What do you do?"
Goal: Make them curious enough to ask a follow-up question.

Format:
"We help [customer type] [achieve outcome] by [key mechanism]. Unlike [alternative], we [differentiator]."

#### 3. Sales Email Opening Line
Audience: Busy prospect who receives 100+ emails/day.
Goal: Open rate — don't pitch yet, just earn the reply.

Format:
"I noticed [relevant trigger]. We help [peer companies] [achieve specific outcome] — is this on your radar?"

Tips:
- Start with their world, not yours
- One specific claim, not a list of features
- Ask a question, not for a meeting

#### 4. Onboarding Welcome Message
Audience: New user who just signed up.
Goal: Set expectations, reduce anxiety, create excitement.

Format:
"Welcome to [Product]. Here's what thousands of [customer type] use it for: [top 3 use cases]. You'll get your first [outcome] in under [time]."

#### 5. Investor Pitch (One Slide)
Audience: Investor evaluating 20 decks that week.
Goal: Make the opportunity feel obvious and large.

Format:
- Market: "[X] companies spend $[Y] every year on [problem]"
- Problem: "The current solutions [key failure]"
- Solution: "[Product] [does X differently], resulting in [quantified outcome]"
- Traction: "[Social proof — revenue, growth, customers]"

#### 6. Tweet / Short Form
Audience: Social followers scrolling fast.
Goal: Stop the scroll; convey value in 280 characters.

Format:
One bold claim + proof point + what you do about it.

### Quality Checklist

Before finalizing any value prop statement:
- [ ] Customer is the subject, not the product
- [ ] Specific outcome stated (not "better" or "faster" alone)
- [ ] Can be understood by a smart 12-year-old
- [ ] Doesn't use words competitors use
- [ ] Has at least one specific, credible detail

## Output Format

Generate value prop statements for all six contexts (or the specific ones requested). For each:
1. The statement itself (ready to use)
2. A 1-sentence note on what makes it effective
3. An A/B variant (alternative version to test)

End with a recommended hierarchy: which statement to use as the primary homepage headline, and how the others support it.
